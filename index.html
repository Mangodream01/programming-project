Variance in happiness through Europe

Happiness is more and more thought of as a useful measure of social progress and, therefore, an important target of public policy. Correspondingly, research to determinants of happiness has been flourishing. On this web page, eleven explanatory variables of life satisfaction in Europe are visualized, which are considered to play an important role in the observed variation in happiness between countries. Importantly, it should be noticed that these variables indicate interesting notions of correlation (both with each other as with unmeasured factors) rather than reflect clean causal relationships.

Parallel coordinates

Parallel coordinates

Parallel coordinates are a means to analyze multivariate data. Since no natural order of axes exists, multiple arrangements are possible. Discover relationships between variables by dragging axes to other positions. A positive relationship between two variables is suggested when lines between two adjacent axes are somewhat parallel to each other, a negative relationship is suggested when most lines cross, and randomly or parallel lines suggest no particular relationship. It is possible to select a region of interest and drag it within one axis. Furthermore, interesting relationships can be viewed more closely in the zoomable scatter plot visualization (next tab).

Scatter plot

Scatter plots demonstrate data values of two variables on Cartesian coordinates. The X and Y variable can be selected in the right-sided dropdown menus. The axes scale dynamically with the new data points. Clicking on a country in the EU data map will highlight the corresponding data point in the scatter plot. Click again to un-highlight. Moreover, when changing year by the time slider, the scatter plot will update and only display the data points in this specific year. Click on years in the legend to display multiple years for comparison (highlighted data points will remain highlighted despite slider updates).
